# Aviation Weather Terminology Glossary

This glossary contains definitions of critical aviation weather terms, abbreviations, and parameters used in flight operations.

### METAR (Meteorological Aerodrome Report)
A routine hourly or half-hourly weather report issued for a specific airport. It contains data such as wind speed and direction, visibility, runway visual range, present weather (e.g. rain, snow), cloud cover and height, temperature, dew point, and altimeter setting.

### TAF (Terminal Aerodrome Forecast)
A weather forecast issued for a specific airport covering a 24-hour or 30-hour period. It uses similar formatting and abbreviations to METAR but includes forecast change indicators like BECMG, TEMPO, and PROB.

### CAVOK (Ceiling and Visibility OK)
An abbreviation used in METARs and TAFs indicating that visibility is 10 km (6 miles) or more, there are no clouds below 5,000 feet (or minimum sector altitude, whichever is greater), no cumulonimbus (CB) or towering cumulus (TCU) clouds, and no significant weather phenomena.

### Wind Shear
A sudden, drastic change in wind speed and/or wind direction over a short distance, either horizontally or vertically. Wind shear can cause sudden loss of airspeed, lift, and altitude, representing a severe safety hazard during takeoff and landing.

### Microburst
A localized, intense column of sinking air (downdraft) that occurs within a thunderstorm. Upon hitting the ground, it spreads out in all directions, creating strong wind shear and severe headwind-to-tailwind transitions for landing/taking off aircraft.

### Ceiling
The height above the Earth's surface of the lowest layer of clouds or obscuring phenomena that is reported as Broken (BKN) or Overcast (OVC), or vertical visibility in an indefinite ceiling (VV). Scatter (SCT) or Few (FEW) layers do not constitute a ceiling.

### Dew Point (Dewpoint)
The temperature to which air must be cooled (at constant pressure) to become saturated with water vapor. When the temperature and dew point spread is small (typically 3°C or less), condensation occurs, resulting in fog, mist, or low clouds.

### Altimeter Setting (QNH)
The barometric pressure setting adjusted to mean sea level. When set on an aircraft's altimeter subscale, it causes the altimeter to display indicate altitude above mean sea level. Expressed in inches of mercury (e.g. 29.92 inHg) or hectopascals/millibars (e.g. 1013 hPa).

### QFE
The barometric pressure setting adjusted to airport elevation. When set on the altimeter subscale, the altimeter will read height above the airport runway (i.e. zero on the ground).

### SIGMET (Significant Meteorological Information)
A weather advisory issued concerning the occurrence or expected occurrence of en-route weather phenomena that may affect the safety of all aircraft operations (e.g. severe turbulence, severe icing, volcanic ash, active thunderstorms).

### AIRMET (Airmen's Meteorological Information)
A weather advisory concerning weather phenomena of lower intensity than those requiring a SIGMET, primarily intended for light or low-level aircraft (e.g. moderate icing, moderate turbulence, widespread ceilings < 1,000 ft or visibility < 3 miles).

### VFR (Visual Flight Rules)
Regulations governing flight operations under weather conditions clear enough to allow pilots to fly by visual reference to the ground and other aircraft. Generally requires a ceiling of at least 1,000 feet and visibility of at least 3 Statute Miles (SM).

### IFR (Instrument Flight Rules)
Regulations governing flight operations under instrument meteorological conditions (IMC), where pilots navigate solely by reference to instruments. Required when weather is below VFR minimums (ceilings < 1,000 feet or visibility < 3 SM).

### MVFR (Marginal Visual Flight Rules)
Weather conditions that are at the lower end of visual rules: ceilings between 1,000 and 3,000 feet, and/or visibility between 3 and 5 Statute Miles (SM).

### LIFR (Low Instrument Flight Rules)
Severely low instrument weather conditions: ceilings less than 500 feet, and/or visibility less than 1 Statute Mile (SM).

### Cumulonimbus (CB)
A heavy, dense cloud of considerable vertical extent in the form of a mountain or huge tower. Associated with thunderstorms, heavy rain, lightning, hail, and severe turbulence/wind shear.

### Towering Cumulus (TCU)
A large cumulus cloud showing great vertical development, indicating strong atmospheric instability and a precursor to a cumulonimbus (CB) cloud.

### BECMG (Becoming)
A change indicator in TAFs specifying a gradual change in weather conditions over a period of time, usually not exceeding 2 hours.

### TEMPO (Temporary)
A change indicator in TAFs specifying temporary fluctuations in weather conditions that are expected to last for less than one hour in each instance and, in the aggregate, cover less than half of the forecast period.

### PROB30 / PROB40 (Probability)
Indicates a 30% or 40% probability of occurrence of temporary or alternate weather conditions during a specified period.

### NOSIG (No Significant Change)
Appended to a METAR trend forecast indicating that no significant change in weather conditions is expected in the next 2 hours.

### Wind Gust
A rapid, temporary fluctuation in wind speed where the peak wind speed exceeds the average wind speed by at least 10 knots.

### Density Altitude
Pressure altitude corrected for non-standard temperature. High temperature and high elevation result in a high density altitude, which reduces engine horsepower, propeller efficiency, and wing lift, thereby increasing takeoff roll and reducing climb performance.
